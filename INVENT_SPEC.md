Determine whether there is a sense of temporal specificity — the feeling that the shutter fired at exactly the right moment and that a fraction of a second in either direction would have produced an entirely different image. Look for transitions between states (waking and sleeping, curiosity and indifference, stillness and motion), peak actions (mid-leap, mid-yawn, mid-stretch), and moments that feel intimate or particular to this specific cat in this specific setting. An image that feels like a portrait of a living individual caught in the middle of its life should rank high. An image that feels like a generic record — a document confirming a cat existed in a location at a time, with no sense of story or specificity — should rank low. The most authentic moments feel almost intimate, as though the viewer has been given access to a private exchange between the cat and its environment.
