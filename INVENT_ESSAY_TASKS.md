Task 1 — Evaluate Temporal Specificity: Examine the cat photograph and judge how precisely timed the captured instant feels. Determine whether the image records a fleeting, unrepeatable moment — such as the peak of an action, a micro-expression, or a split-second of bodily tension — or whether it depicts a stable, persistent state that could have been photographed at any point across a longer stretch of time and produced an essentially identical image. The core question is: if this photograph had been taken one second earlier or later, would it look meaningfully different? Score higher when the answer is emphatically yes — when the image feels urgent and almost lucky in its timing — and lower when the moment shown is interchangeable with the seconds surrounding it.

Task 2 — Evaluate Transitional Vitality: Examine the cat photograph and judge the degree to which the cat is caught in a state of becoming rather than a settled state of being. Look for evidence that the cat is visibly between two conditions — between stillness and motion, between sleep and wakefulness, between curiosity and indifference, between composure and surprise. This includes physical transitions such as a body mid-stretch, a head in the act of turning, or weight shifting unevenly between paws, as well as emotional and attentional transitions such as awareness flooding into a newly opened eye or a playful cat freezing into sudden uncertainty. Score higher when the image vibrates with implied before-and-after — when you can sense what the cat was just doing and what it is about to do — and lower when the cat appears fixed in a single, stable condition with no visible evidence of change or process.

Task 3 — Evaluate Particular Intimacy: Examine the cat photograph and judge whether the image feels like a portrait of this specific cat in this specific life, or whether it could be a generic photograph of any cat anywhere. Look for specificity of detail that reveals individuality: posture that suggests personality or habit, a relationship between the cat and its environment that implies familiarity and belonging, a gaze that feels directed and particular rather than vacant, and contextual details that anchor the moment in a real, lived-in world. The core question is: does this image introduce you to an individual creature and imply a story about its day, its preferences, and its private life, or does it merely confirm that a cat was present? Score higher when the photograph feels like an intimate encounter with a particular being, and lower when the cat and its surroundings feel interchangeable and anonymous.
